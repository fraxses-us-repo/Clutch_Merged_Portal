HIPAA Authorization to Use and Disclose Protected Health Information

This Authorization Form ("Authorization") describes different uses and disclosures of health information, including as protected under applicable state and provincial law and also "protected health information" as defined by the federal Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA") and the regulations promulgated thereunder. This authorization shall remain in effect for three (3) years from the date of signature unless revoked by you via the method set forth below.

I hereby authorize Clutch Diagnostics LLC ("Clutch") to use and/or disclose the protected health information about me collected by Clutch ("PHI") to Clutch’s designated laboratory partner facilities, including LabCorp and its affiliates. This PHI may be used and/or disclosed to conduct the collection, testing, and analysis for the purposes of a COVID-19 PCR diagnostic test.

I understand that, as set forth in the notice of privacy practices, I have the right to revoke this authorization, in writing, at any time, except to the extent that Clutch has acted in reliance upon it, by sending written notification to Clutch at patientservices@clutch.health or at the address below:

Clutch Diagnostics LLC
438 Porsche Avenue
Atlanta, GA 30354

Attn: Privacy Officer

I understand that the provision of treatment by Clutch which is connected to or generates the health record/PHI disclosed under this Authorization is conditioned on my execution of this Authorization. I understand such treatment by Clutch is solely for the purpose of creating the above identified health record/PHI for disclosure to Clutch’s designated laboratory partner facilities. I further understand that if I do not execute this Authorization, the sample I provide via the Clutch Self-Collection Kit will not be sent to Clutch’s designated laboratory partner facilities for the purposes of conducting a COVID-19 PCR diagnostic test.

I understand that PHI used or disclosed pursuant to this authorization may be redisclosed by the recipient and its confidentiality may no longer be protected by federal or state law.
